The PWG held its November 2019 Virtual Face-to-Face Meeting on November 20-21, 2019 via WebEx teleconferences. Representatives from Apple, Canon, Google, High North, HP Inc., Kyocera, Lexmark, TIC, TCS and Xerox attended the meetings. Attendees reviewed work in progress, including drafts of a number of in-progress specifications, and discussed liaisons with partner groups. Here is a summary of the proceedings.

PWG Plenary

As in previous PWG Face to Face events, the PWG Chair presented the Plenary. We reviewed the overall state of the PWG, its programs and initiatives, and briefly discussed upcoming face-to-face meeting scheduling. We noted that there are now 412 printers certified under the PWG's IPP Everywhere™ Self Certification program, with more certifications believed to be in progress. We discussed the PWG Steering Committee's activities and initiatives, including PR efforts, recently approved documents, process document updates, the website migration initiative, and "toolset" alternatives for producing specifications. Each active PWG workgroup (IDS Workgroup and IPP Workgroup) summarized their current status. Finally, we reviewed the status of our partners' work in Trusted Computing Group (TCG), IETF, Linux Foundation OpenPrinting Workgroup, Mopria Alliance and 3MF Consortium.

Complete minutes are available here: https://ftp.pwg.org/pub/pwg/general/minutes/pwg-plenary-minutes-20191120.htm

Internet Printing Protocol (IPP) Workgroup

On the first day, Ira McDonald (IPP WG Co-Chair) and Mike Sweet (IPP WG Secretary) surveyed the status of current IPP Workgroup works in progress. We then briefly reviewed the status of IPP Everywhere™ v1.1 and IPP Everywhere Self Certification v1.1, noting that a 1.0 Update 4 certification test suite would be made available soon. We then discussed a PWG presentation authored by Mike Sweet for the UK GovPrint Initiative, pointing out the availability of open technologies from the PWG as possible solutions. After a break, we briefly discussed the status of IPP System Service, which was in PWG Formal Approval completing on November 22, 2019 and was approved as PWG 5100.22, to be published soon. We then completed a full review of the latest draft of IPP Enterprise Printing Extensions v2.0, which ran over. We finished the day with IPP Production Printing Extensions v2.0, noting some minor normative language changes and an addition for roll feed printers. We deferred review of IPP Driverless Printing Extensions until the following day.

On the second day, after the IDS Workgroup session, the IPP Workgroup sessions started with a review of IPP Driverless Printing Extensions v2.0, during what was originally scheduled to be the IPP Encrypted Jobs and Documents v1.0 session. (Since that document hadn't been updated since its last review, further discussion of that document was postponed). The document wasn't fully reviewed but good progress was made, and the review would be completed at the next IPP WG teleconference. We then shifted to Job Accounting with IPP v1.0, and had some very productive discussions about security and use models. After a break, Paul Tykodi led a discussion on the status of the PWG's 3D Printing related liaisons and outreach efforts, noting that we were in the process of establishing a liaison with INCITS. We concluded the IPP WG's sessions with next steps.

Complete minutes are available here: https://ftp.pwg.org/pub/pwg/ipp/minutes/ippv2-f2f-minutes-20191120.pdf

Imaging Device Security (IDS) Workgroup

At the start of the second day, Alan Sukert led the IDS Workgroup status and progress discussion. It was mentioned that FIPS 140-3 will become required on all cryptographic modules in the US on or after September 22, 2021, and we discussed how that will affect Common Criteria certification after that date. We also discussed what will happen after the IEEE sunsets the two 2600 Protection Profiles (PPs) on Dec 31, 2019. In particular, there was concern about whether the specifications themselves would continue to be available. That would seem to put pressure on the HCD TC to get the HCD cPP v1.0 done as quickly as possible once it becomes an HCD international TC (iTC). Status of that initiative was then discussed. The IDS WG members are hoping to drive an aggressive schedule in the iTC once it is formed. The rollout timeline and additional needs were discussed. Ira and Alan briefly discussed the status of the PWG HCD Security Guide draft and some proposed additions. We wrapped up with next steps.
