The PWG held its February 2019 Virtual Face-to-Face Meeting on February 14-15, 2019 via WebEx teleconferences. Representatives from Apple, Brother, Canon, Google, High North, HP Inc., Kyocera, Lexmark, Oki Data, TIC, TCS and Xerox attended the meetings. Attendees reviewed work in progress, including drafts of a number of in-progress specifications, and discussed liaisons with partner groups. Here is a summary of the proceedings.

PWG Plenary

In the PWG Plenary, we reviewed the overall state of the PWG, its programs and initiatives, and briefly discussed upcoming face-to-face meeting scheduling. We noted that there are now 355 printers certified by the PWG's IPP Everywhere™ Self Certification program, with more certifications believed to be in progress. Each active PWG workgroup (IDS Workgroup and IPP Workgroup) summarized their current status. Finally, we reviewed the status of our partners' work in Trusted Computing Group (TCG), IETF, Linux Foundation OpenPrinting Workgroup and Mopria Alliance.

Complete minutes are available here: https://ftp.pwg.org/pub/pwg/general/minutes/pwg-plenary-minutes-20190213.htm

Internet Printing Protocol (IPP) Workgroup

On the first day, Ira McDonald and Mike Sweet surveyed the status of current IPP Workgroup work in progress. That initial session included an extended discussion of errata updates, deprecation and obsolescence of attributes that were judged to be non-interoperable and might be replaced. Other process and template changes were considered, including an "Obsolescence Rationale". An "IPP Design Patterns" document was again discussed, and it was suggested that we pursue that first in the GitHub "istopwg" wiki. We also discussed requiring prototyping and/or requesting intents to implement, to hopefully prevent specifying IPP extensions that are not practically implementable in the future. We concluded this segment after considering the changes to the website and the IPP Everywhere™ Self Certification submission process. This discussion ran over into the session where the current stable draft of IPP Everywhere v1.1 and IPP Everywhere Printer Self-Certification Manual v1.1 were to be reviewed, but these documents are stable and were not looked at again in this event. We briefly discussed the latest drafts of IPP 3D Printing Extensions v1.1 and PWG Safe G-Code. Both have completed IPP WG Last Call. IPP 3D Printing Extensions v1.1 will have its PWG Call for Objections in Q1 2019. PWG Safe G-Code will complete its PWG Last Call Feb. 28, 2019, with a PWG Formal Vote in Q1 2019. We then reviewed IPP System Service and briefly talked about the status of IPP Authentication Methods, which is in PWG Last Call.

On the second day, the IPP Workgroup sessions started with a presentation and update on IPP Job and Printer Extensions Set 2 v2.0 (JPS2v2) by Smith Kennedy (HP Inc.) examining curiosities with IPP job states / job phases, and the semantics of IPP Proof Print, IPP Job Save and Reprint, and the newly proposed IPP Job Storage features. After a lively discussion critiquing the structure and content of PWG 5100.11-2010 (JPS2 v1), it was proposed that we obsolete all of PWG 5100.11-2010, and create a different document (or documents) that will keep what is worthy of keeping and obsolete what is not. Further discussion of this effort will occur at the next IPP WG teleconference on Feb. 28, 2019. We reviewed the most recent draft of MFD Alerts v1.1, judging it to be ready for IPP WG Last Call. We briefly reviewed the most recent draft of IPP Document Object v1.1, which was also ready for IPP WG Last Call, and concluded with next steps.

Complete minutes are available here: https://ftp.pwg.org/pub/pwg/ipp/minutes/ippv2-f2f-minutes-20190213.pdf

Imaging Device Security (IDS) Workgroup

On the morning of the second day, Alan Sukert led the IDS Workgroup session in a review of comments against the final draft of the proposed HCD PP v1.1 and the latest status on efforts to create an HCD international Technical Committee (iTC) tasked with transforming HCD PP v1.1 into an HCD collaborative PP (cPP). We also reviewed the comments against the latest drafts of the Essential Security Requirements (ESR) document and the Terms of Reference (ToR) documents, as well as the actual draft ESR itself, that are being created to be submitted to the Common Criteria Development Board (CCDB) for approval; once these two documents are approved by the CCDB we can formally initiate the HCD iTC. IDS members present provided some good additional comments against the ESR and ToR that will be submitted for consideration by the HCD TC at its next Conference Call in March 2019. Finally, we discussed the proposed contents and timelines for approval of the HCD PP v1.1 and an eventual HCD cPP v1.0. The IDS Workgroup will hold another Conference Call in March 2019 to review the proposed ToR document in more detail and discuss actions to initiate a dialogue to establish a liaison with the TCG.
