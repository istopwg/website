Meeting Planning Guidelines

The PWG operates its meeting by having volunteers to plan, organize and run the logistics of the meetings.  Over time, the group has learned how to deal with the contracts and others details of the hotels.  The following checklist should serve as a handy aid in planning the meeting logistics.

Hotel Selection Criteria

1) The hotel should ideally be situated in a location where after meeting activities can be done with relative ease.  Locations that are miles from restaurants, etc.are often less than ideal.  Locations with good access to public transportation and good access to the airport through a free shuttle are especially good.  Additionally, the location should have convenient places where lunch can be had in a 1 to 1.5 hour lunch break.

2) The hotel must have full air conditioning and heating.  (The PWG's meetings prove Murphy's law by holding meeting in places that should be cool and the weather turns out to be hot and vice versa.)  It is recommended that the hotel offer a full range of services including faxing, room service, parking, etc.

3) The hotel shall provide high quality analog modem capable telephone lines in each sleeping room.  High speed internet connection via ethernet is nice but not required.

The PWG's Needs during the Meeting

1) Morning food should consist roughly of a selection of juices, pastries and fruit in addition to regular and decaf coffee.  Hot water and a selection of teas should be offered.  This food must remain accessible until the noon break.  A mid-morning refresh with more coffee and the addition of ice and soft drinks is also nice to do.

2) An afternoon break consisting of coffee/tea as well as ice and cold beverages (soft drinks, bottled water, etc.) must be ordered.  The food of this afternoon break can be just about anything with broad appeal including:

  • cookies, brownies or other "sweets"
  • chips, pretzels and other salty treats
  • ice cream sundaes
  • ice cream bars
  • etc.

There should be NO deviation from #1 and #2 above for any reason including local cultural differences or preferences.  By providing these services, the needs of all the participants from around the world can be met.

3) The hotel should provide seating for the number of attendees planned in a "U" shaped configuration.  In addition, an overhead projector and screen should also be ordered unless one or both are being provided by a PWG participant.   Electrical outlets for laptops should also be provided and be located in the center of the "U" shaped area.

4) Meeting charges to cover the meeting room, food and A/V items should normally be in the $40 per day range.

Hotel Contracts

1) The hotels like to lock you into a contract with no means of escape should something happen to change or cancel the meeting.  As such, it is not recommended that you agree to immediate "liquidated damages" should the meeting be canceled.  Instead, offer liquidated damages only if the meeting is canceled and NOT rescheduled within 6 to 12  months.

2) Hotels have a lot of flexibility in what they charge for the meeting room.  It is usually quite possible to negotiated the price down into the $100 per day range.   Don't accept the first price they offer.

3) The hotel contract should allow you to specify the amount of food to be provided as close as possible to the actual meeting date.  Normally it is sufficient to order food for about 80%-90% of the number of people attending.  Be sure the hotel knows that the number of chairs to be in the room is one number and the amount of food to be provided is another number.  It is alway better to have more chairs and table space available than the number of signed up participants would suggest.  Most participants are using laptops and shuffling papers so having them sit shoulder to shoulder around a crowded table is not productive.

4) Negotiate the cut-off date for registration with the hotel to be as late, i.e. as close to the meeting date, as possible

5) Estimate the number of rooms needed to be 20 per night from Sunday until Thursday night.  Friday night should be about 5.  Make sure the contract doesn't commit you to these numbers.

Participant Payment

1) At this time, Larry Stein of Warp 9 has the ability to provided a web based payment method to cover the cost of the meeting room, food, etc.  Contact him before the meeting with the per day charge number.  Make sure you have a plan with Larry to be paid or have the hotel paid from the monies collected by the web page service.

2) Alternatively, if the hotel is willing to do so, meeting charges can be accumulated by the hotel and charged to the participant's room or credit card.

Before the Meeting

1) Notify the PWG Chair (don@lexmark.com) of the hotel selected with the details for publication on the Web Page

2) Send a note to the PWG-ANNOUNCE@PWG.ORG mailing list with that information and asking for attendees to "ping" you with their meeting attendance plans.  Include a pointer to the meeting payment web page if you are using that service from Larry Stein.

3) Keep a list of those attending and several weeks before the meeting publish that list to the mailing list above.

4) Make sure all catering and meeting room details are closed with the hotel a week before the meeting.

During the Meeting

1) Work with the hotel on any issues or deficiencies that arrive during the course of the week.  (If you are not there the entire week, please arrange for someone else to do this during your absence.)
