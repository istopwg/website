IPP Frequently Asked Questions

What is IPP Everywhere?

IPP Everywhere is an exciting new version of IPP that makes it possible to print to network printers without special software. You can find out more on the IPP Everywhere technology page.

What Computers Support IPP Everywhere?

Any computer running CUPS 1.5 or later supports IPP Everywhere. For now that basically means all major Linux distributions, Open Solaris, *BSD, and OS X.

What Mobile Devices Support IPP Everywhere?

Mobile device support is still under development.

What Printers Support IPP Everywhere?

Printers supporting the final IPP Everywhere specification should start appearing later in 2014 and early 2015.

What About USB Printers?

The USB-IF recently approved a new IPP USB Specification that allows printers to support IPP Everywhere over USB. Printers supporting IPP USB should start appearing later in 2013 and early 2014.

What About Wi-Fi Direct?

The Wi-Fi Alliance is currently developing a Wi-Fi Direct Printing specification, based in large part upon IPP Everywhere.

What About Scanning, Facsimile, Etc?

The PWG IPP workgroup is currently developing specifications for scanning, facsimile, and other multifunction device services. These will be combined with IPP Everywhere to create the IPP Multifunction specification to support accessing all of the services of a multifunction printer without special software.

Where Can I Learn More About IPP Everywhere?

The current specifications, mailing list, and archives for IPP Everywhere are available on the PWG IPP workgroup page.
